## Cross-Category Techniques

- Start with product visibility, not narrative setup.
- Use 9:16 vertical framing and keep the product centered or hand-held near the lens.
- Alternate context shots and proof shots: hero view -> detail label/texture -> use/action -> result.
- Make motion simple and legible: walk, turn, pick up, open, pour, wipe, compare, place on desk.
- Keep backgrounds category-coded: fashion street/mirror/outdoor, beauty saturated studio color, home tabletop/bedroom, food unpacking/pour, digital desk setup.
- Use short overlay copy as evidence labels, not long paragraphs.
- Prefer 10-18 second loops with 4-7 meaningful beats.

## Structured Asset Requirements

- For every ingested video, keep a video-level summary for recall and a slice-level record for each usable shot.
- For apparel, product dimensions should capture subject (`outfit`, `top`, `skirt`, `shoe`), category (`服饰鞋包`), usage scene (`street`, `mirror try-on`, `outdoor walk`), and claim constraints (`do not invent brand/price/body-effect claims`).
- For beauty, home, food, and digital examples, slice dimensions should preserve the exact proof action: packshot, label, texture, hands-on demo, pour/open, wipe/use, or device operation.
- Script modules should consume slice `usage_context` and `script_role` rather than the raw original video.

## Apparel Techniques

- Full-body reveal in the first frame.
- Pose or walking sequence to show fit, drape, silhouette, and lifestyle context.
- Mirror or fixed-camera composition for try-on credibility.
- Outdoor walking creates lifestyle aspiration; indoor mirror creates buying confidence.
- Reusable recipe: full outfit reveal -> closer top/detail -> side/back/turn -> lifestyle walk or pose -> CTA.

## Beauty Techniques

- Strong product hero color and macro packshot.
- Brand/series copy appears as an aesthetic layer.
- Reusable recipe: beauty color field -> product cluster -> single product close-up -> texture/result claim -> final hero.

## Home Techniques

- Hands demonstrate texture or function.
- Packaging and multiple units establish inventory and trust.
- Reusable recipe: product pile/context -> hand close-up -> use demo -> result surface -> packshot.

## Food Techniques

- Unboxing and label close-up establish authenticity.
- Pour/open/eat action is the proof.
- Reusable recipe: box reveal -> single item close-up -> open/pour -> appetizing result -> origin/flavor cue.

## Digital Techniques

- Packaging reveal and device close-up show configuration.
- Product remains on desk where use context is obvious.
- Reusable recipe: box/plan reveal -> device hero -> feature detail -> hand interaction -> desk result.
