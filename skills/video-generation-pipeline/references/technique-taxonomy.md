# Technique Taxonomy

Classify every extracted technique into these buckets.

| Bucket | What To Capture | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hook | First 0-3 seconds, attention trigger, curiosity gap | full outfit reveal, unboxing top-down, product hero with bold claim |
| Product Evidence | What proves the product exists and works | label close-up, texture shot, try-on, pour test, hands-on use |
| Camera And Framing | How the viewer sees the product | full-body mirror, handheld walk-up, top-down table, macro close-up |
| Motion | What changes across frames | turn, walk toward camera, open package, apply product, pour/use/test |
| Scene Design | Environment and props | street, bedroom mirror, red beauty set, tabletop, kitchen, desk setup |
| Copy And OCR | On-screen words and caption behavior | short white caption, vertical brand slogan, label close-up |
| Audio | Voice, music, sound effects | soft BGM, upbeat cut rhythm, product-action sounds |
| Editing Rhythm | Shot length and transition logic | 1-2 second cuts, fixed-camera pose sequence, demo sequence |
| CTA | Conversion cue | show result, mention use case, encourage click/save/buy |
| Risks | Things to avoid or clean | watermark, unreadable text, exaggerated claims, low product visibility |

Confidence rule:

- High: appears in 3+ references or one official/intentional campaign sample.
- Medium: appears in 2 references or is strongly supported by one detailed analysis.
- Low: appears once; mark as candidate, not a rule.
